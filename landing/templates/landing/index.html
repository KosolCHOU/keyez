FAQ

What problem does KeyEZ solve?

Cambodian Gen Z increasingly uses SingKhmer (Latin Khmer) for texting, which causes a decline in real Khmer script usage. KeyEZ solves this by converting SingKhmer to proper Khmer instantly — making Khmer typing fast, natural, and accessible again.

How does KeyEZ work?

It uses AI transliteration — a Seq2Seq Transformer model trained on thousands of SingKhmer–Khmer pairs — to map Latin sounds to correct Khmer letters. It also features a handwriting canvas that recognizes drawn Khmer letters and turns them into clean digital text.

Who is KeyEZ for?

KeyEZ is built for students, creators, and young professionals aged 13–30 who often type in SingKhmer on social media or chat apps but want to write properly in Khmer without slowing down.

Does KeyEZ need internet to work?

No. The Gold version runs fully offline using compact on-device models. The Free version may use online suggestions for better accuracy.

How does KeyEZ support education in Cambodia?

KeyEZ helps students practice and master Khmer spelling, grammar, and handwriting through real-time transliteration and canvas writing. It promotes Khmer literacy and identity while fitting naturally into digital learning environments.
